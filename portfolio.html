Deepwater Subsea · Gas Development

Project Overview

RROC supported the client from FEED through execution and start-up of a 1600 m deepwater SURF (Subsea, Umbilicals, Risers & Flowlines) gas development in Asia, providing technical leadership and operational coordination across the full SURF scope — subsea infrastructure, drilling operations and a Floating Production Unit (FPU).

The Challenge

Deliver a safe, efficient start-up strategy for a 1600 m deepwater SURF gas development with complex coordination between subsea infrastructure, drilling operations and the FPU — all while managing multiple subcontractor interfaces and maintaining an uncompromising safety posture.

RROC's Approach — Phased Execution

1
Start-up Strategy & Selection

Developed and selected the optimum start-up strategy for deepwater SURF operations balancing schedule, resource availability and integrated-system risk.

2
Subcontractor & Interface Management

Managed subcontractor scopes and execution interfaces during SURF pre-commissioning, ensuring clear accountability and procedure compliance.

3
Subsea XT Well Commissioning

Led subsea Christmas Tree (XT) well commissioning with integrated controls, barrier verification and flow assurance checks.

4
Integrated Drilling–FPU Start-up

Coordinated the integrated start-up between drilling rig and FPU — aligning communications, interlocks and test envelopes across parties.

5
Subsea & Topside Integration

Ensured safe system integration between subsea and topside facilities, including joint tests, performance tests and handover into steady-state production.

Results & Impact

✓
Production start-up achieved weeks ahead of schedule — strategy optimisation reduced critical-path exposure.
✓
Zero Lost Time Incidents (LTI) across the entire FEED-to-start-up campaign.
✓
High client satisfaction based on delivery certainty, transparent reporting and proactive risk management.
✓
Clean subsea–topsides handover with documented test records and traceable compliance evidence.

Offshore Renewables · Heavy Lift

Project Overview

ClientConfidential (UK / Asia)
ScopeLoad-Out Supervision · Lift Plan Review · Heavy Lift Operations
AssetsOffshore Wind Turbine Components, Plates, Installation Grippers, Piles

Our client called on RROC at a critical time — driven by contractual challenges — to oversee the load-out of offshore wind farm turbine components and ancillaries from the UK to Asia for fabrication. RROC's experienced MWS personnel worked alongside the client's engineers, logistics team and the designated port captain to deliver a safe, compliant operation.

The Challenge

Resolve a contractually-pressured load-out of multiple OWF components — plates, installation grippers, piles and turbine parts — onto a Heavy Lift Vessel for sail-away, while maintaining full lift-plan compliance and auditable sea-fastening certification.

RROC's Approach

1
Lift Plan & Strategy Review

Evaluated and approved all lift plans, rigging procedures and equipment condition with the client's engineers and port captain.

2
Sea-Fastening & Certification

Verified sea-fastening, lashing arrangements and certification compliance against international heavy-lift standards.

3
Back-Office Design Support

Back-office design and analysis engineers approved lifting designs and resolved technical queries raised at the quayside.

4
Quayside-to-HLV Heavy Lift Execution

Managed the quayside-to-HLV transfer using Sarens heavy lift & transportation equipment, onshore cranes and HLV cranes — lifting plates, installation grippers, piles and turbine components ready for sail-away.

5
Collaboration & Coordination

Seamless collaboration between RROC MWS, client engineers, logistics providers and port authorities throughout the campaign.

Results & Impact

✓
All OWF components successfully loaded for sail-away.
✓
Zero incidents during quayside and HLV operations.
✓
Full compliance with lift plan and certification requirements.
✓
Strong client satisfaction for safe, efficient execution under commercial pressure.

FPSO · Topsides Modifications · Subsea Tie-Back

Project Overview

ClientConfidential (West Africa)
Project PhasePre-Offshore Tie-Back Execution
ScopeTopsides & Subsea Modifications · Pre-Commissioning Support · Subcontractor Supervision

On behalf of our client in West Africa, RROC supervised subcontractors and coordinated mechanical, electrical, instrumentation and subsea modifications on the FPSO topsides ahead of an offshore subsea tie-back execution campaign — ensuring all modifications and pre-commissioning activities were completed efficiently and safely to support the offshore campaign and SURF integrated commissioning.

The Challenge

A tight start-up schedule and offshore campaign window required all topsides modifications, pre-commissioning and subsea-controls work to be completed on time, safely, and with seamless interface to the SURF integrated commissioning.

Key Scope of Work

Procedure & Plan Review

Reviewed and approved all subcontractor procedures and task plans before execution.

Chemical Injection & TUTU

Chemical Injection Module and TUTU expansion delivered with full tie-in testing.

New Piping Installation

New piping installation and pre-commissioning with hydrotest, reinstatement and system handover.

SPCU Subsea Controls

Subsea controls SPCU installation & commissioning, aligned with subsea network design.

AVGM Operations

Annulus Vent Gas Monitoring operations coordinated within the broader topsides programme.

Results & Impact

✓
All topsides and subsea modifications completed on schedule.
✓
Seamless transition to offshore execution and SURF integrated commissioning.
✓
Start-up targets met successfully with high client satisfaction.
✓
Strong safety and compliance oversight across subcontractors and disciplines.
